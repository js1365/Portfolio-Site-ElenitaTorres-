Work

UX/UI Case Study Responsive Web App: Recipes

The Process

Why Recipes

I chose this for a few reasons, firstly I do love to cook. I really got into cooking when my daughter was born. The crazy part is that my daughter had a lot of health issues as she is allergic to 70% of all foods. So I was constantly trying to finding ways to be able to feed her properly without her having a reaction. Secondly, I found it frustrating to find recipes that I would love to cook but she was allergic to them, however with that I started finding ways to substitute ingredients so that she could eat, but these were not in any of the sites I ever went into.

Solution

I thought I would work on a recipe app combining two things. One, spotlighting fun healthy recipes that show alternatives to ingredients on the recipes. Two, I thought I would focus on Filipino food recipes, I think underserved market.

My Role

UI / UX Designer User Research Personas User Flow on MVP & JTBD Low,Mid, High Fidelity Wireframes

Style Guide Logo UI Elements Color Palette Typography Feedback

Duration

Overall: 2 months

Tools

Research

Competitive Analysis

There was a Competitive Analysis created between the competitors Tasty and Food Network. By comparing their strengths, weaknesses, opportunities and threats it brought out their key objectives and strategy. his was combined with the UX Analysis for both companies.

User Research

From the competitive analysis, interviews were conducted. A hypothesis was created and questions was written, interviewing 3 people from 3 different target markets

Hypothesis

Users that want to try new recipes from a different country. They may or may not have dietary issues. They are interested in trying out new flavors. These Users want to keep things simple and easy to follow recipes, this would be accomplished by keeping the instructions in a simplified manner. Listing ingredient alternatives, prep time, how long it will take to cook, how many servings, nutrition info, and saving the recipe. An area where the User could add it onto their shopping list. There would be an area where the User would see how good the recipe is, how many reviews the recipe has had and there would be video on how to make the dish.

What did I find from the interviews

Goals & Needs

• To see few recipes • Substitutions list is important when alterations for dietary issues like lactose intolerant or celiac • Taste factor

Frustrations

• Scrolling too long to find a recipe negatively impacts the desire to make the dish • When the site has too long of a personal story again makes a negative impact on her desire to try the recipe. • If there are poor ingredients they will pass on the recipe.
